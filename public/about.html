About us

We're building the commerce layer for African WhatsApp.

Hundreds of millions of people in West Africa already buy and sell on WhatsApp. They lose hours every day to manual order-taking, missed payments, and forgotten follow-ups. WA-B exists to fix that — quietly, reliably, and in cedis.

The story.

WA-B started in 2024 in a kebab shop in Osu. The owner, Auntie Ama, was juggling 18 orders on three phones. Her best customer had just bounced because she missed his message during the lunch rush. We built the first version of WA-B that weekend — a single Python script that took her order, charged MoMo, and printed a receipt.

A year later we'd outgrown the script. The platform now runs hundreds of merchants across Accra, Kumasi and Takoradi. Auntie Ama is customer #1 and still on the Kiosk plan.

We're a small team — six engineers, a designer, two operations leads. We're not VC-funded. We're profitable, and we plan to stay that way.

What we believe.

Boring is beautiful.

The most exciting thing payment infrastructure can do is be invisible. We optimize for predictable, not novel.

Local first.

We design for the Ghanaian SME first. The "global SaaS" patterns don't fit a market that runs on cedis, MoMo, and WhatsApp.

Profit, not vanity.

We charge fair prices and run the business to break even or better. No growth-at-all-costs, no investor pressure, no surprise enshittification.

Documents over meetings.

If a decision matters, write it down. If it doesn't, ship it. We have one weekly meeting and an aggressive doc culture.

Customers are colleagues.

Every engineer rides shotgun on customer calls. The product team eats jollof with merchants once a quarter.

Africa builds the rails.

The infrastructure for African commerce will be built in Africa, by people who use it. We're hiring locally — and proudly.
